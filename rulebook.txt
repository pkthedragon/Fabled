INTRODUCTION
Fabled is a tactical turn-based combat game focused on positioning, ability timing, and party optimization. Players use parties of six random adventurers and choose three to take to encounters to take turns attacking enemies. The encounter is won when the final enemy adventurer is defeated or a player's third Ultimate Spell has been cast.

PARTIES
Players use a party of six adventurers with set loadouts per quest. Only three adventurers are brought to any given encounter.
Adventurers
Adventurers have the following attributes:
An Innate Skill.
Two Signature Weapons.
An Ultimate Spell.
Adventurers also have four stats:
HP, or how much health the adventurer has.
Attack, which affects how much damage the adventurer deals with Strikes.
Defense, which affects how much damage the adventurer takes from abilities.
Speed, which affects initiative.
In addition, adventurers have the following chosen attributes (their loadout):
Primary Weapon.
A Class, and Class Skill.
An Artifact.
Skills and Spells
Skills are passive effects and Spells are effects that use up an action. 
A variety of sources give adventurers access to Skills and Spells, and there is no limit to how many of either an adventurer can have.
Weapons
Weapons determine the strength of an adventurer's Strike, and can come with Skills and/or Spells. They can be Melee, Ranged, or Magic, and their classification determines their usage in encounters.
Adventurers have two Signature Weapons. Before the encounter, they choose one to become their primary weapon and one to become their secondary weapon. Only the primary weapon's Skills and Spells are active.
Classes
The six classes are:
Wardens: Tanks and position manipulators.
Fighters: Melee damage dealers.
Mages: Magic damage dealers.
Rangers: Ranged damage dealers.
Clerics: Healers and enchanters.
Rogues: Disruptors and speedsters.
Classes determine which Class Skill an adventurer can choose from and which Artifacts they are able to attune to.
A party can only have one of each Class among its adventurers.
Artifacts
Artifacts raise a specific stat and give a Spell to the adventurer that holds them. The player can only have one of each artifact.
Artifacts can have Reactive Spells, which are not actions but go on cooldown once triggered. A Reactive Spell being Triggered counts as casting the spell.
Each Artifact has one or more classes that are able to attune to it. If an Artifact is not attuned, its Spell cannot be used.
Adventurers do not need to hold an Artifact.

ENCOUNTERS
Encounters consist of rounds in which every adventurer acts at least once.
Encounter Formation
Each party uses a triangle formation with a backline behind a frontline. Both frontlines face each other with their backlines behind them.
Player A Backline Left				Player A Backline Right
Player A Frontline
Player B Frontline
Player B Backline Left				Player B Backline Right
Formation Rules
Backline adventurers have -30 Speed.
One adventurer must always be in the frontline, and up to two adventurers can be in the backline.
If the frontline adventurer is knocked out, the leftmost backline adventurer moves forward.
Round Order
Initiative Check
Each adventurer's Speed is checked to determine initiative order.
Start of Round Effects
Start of Round effects are resolved in initiative order.
Action Selection
Players select one action for each adventurer in their party.
Resolve Actions
Each adventurer's selected action is resolved in initiative order.
Bonus Action Selection
Players select one bonus action for each adventurer in their party who has one.
Resolve Bonus Actions
Each adventurer's selected bonus action is resolved in initiative order.
End of Round Effects
End of Round and "Each Round" effects are resolved in initiative order.
Action Types
An adventurer may choose one of five actions:
Strike with their primary weapon.
Spellcast.
Switch their primary and secondary weapons .
Swap positions with an ally adventurer.
Skip action.
Strike
To Strike, an adventurer attacks a target with their primary weapon. There are three types of Strikes:
Melee
Melee weapons can only be used in the frontline, and can only target frontline enemies.
Ranged
Ranged weapons can target either the enemy frontline, or a backline enemy directly across from the attacker.  They use up Ammo with each Strike, and once all Ammo is used up, they cannot be used to Strike.
Magic
Magic weapons can target enemies directly across/facing the attacker and the enemy frontline. Striking with a Magic weapon counts as casting a Spell, and they have a cooldown.
Damage uses the following formula:
Damage = Power × (Attack / Defense), rounded up
Damaging an enemy lowers its HP by the damage amount. An enemy is knocked out when it takes damage that reduces its HP to 0.
Spread Strikes damage all legal targets, but the damage is halved.
Spell
To Spellcast, an adventurer uses a Spell they have access to. Spells that target enemies can target enemies directly across the attacker and the enemy frontline. Spells that target allies can target any ally. Once a spell is used, it goes on cooldown.
Switch
To Switch, an adventurer switches their primary and secondary weapon. All cooldowns relating to either weapon are reset, and Ranged weapons fully reload Ammo.
Swap
To Swap, an adventurer swaps positions with an ally. If the ally that didn't select Swap as their action hasn't resolved their action yet, they resolve it from their new position.
Skip
To Skip, an adventurer skips their action.
Ultimate Spell
Whenever an adventurer Strikes or uses a Spell, they fill up the Ultimate Meter by +1 or +2 respectively. Once the Meter is full at +7, one adventurer can use their Ultimate Spell, which depletes the meter.
CONDITIONS
Status conditions of the same type do not stack, but an adventurer can have multiple different status conditions at one time.
Burn: Burned adventurers take 8% max HP damage each round.
Root: Rooted adventurers cannot use the Swap Positions action.
Shock: Shocked adventurers have -15 speed and their Strikes have 15% recoil.
Weaken: Weakened adventurers deal 15% less damage.
Expose: Exposed adventurers take 15% more damage.
Guard: Guarded adventurers take 15% less damage.
Spotlight: Spotlighted adventurers can be targeted by Melee Strikes in the backline.
Taunt: Taunted adventurers can only target whoever taunted them.
Stat bonuses and penalties do not stack. Stat calculation uses the highest stat bonus and the highest stat penalty an adventurer has.
ENDING THE GAME
The game ends immediately upon reaching one of two conditions: the player that casts their 3rd Ultimate Spell will immediately win the game, and the player loses the game immediately when all of their party's adventurers are knocked out.

QUESTS
Quests are competitive runs in which the player continues facing encounters with a chosen party until they've lost three encounters.
Players can pick one favored adventurer they've quested with previously. When starting a Quest, players are shown a pool of nine random adventurers including their favorite, and must create a party of six and set their loadouts.
Encounters
Before starting an encounter, players can alter their party's loadouts. Then before the encounter begins, players see the opponent's party before deciding which three adventurers to bring and what their positions are.
Winning an encounter gives the player Exp, Gold, and Glory, but losing an encounter only takes Glory.
Gold
Gold gain increases with current Quest winstreak. The formula is: 
100 + (10 × current winstreak before win)
Once a player's Quest ends, they get 150 Gold if they won at least five encounters, increased to 300 if they won at least ten.
Glory
Glory affects the player's rank.
Glory gain increases with current Quest winstreak. The formula is:
GloryGain = round(BaseChange + WinstreakBonus), clamps from 8 to 30
BaseChange = 28 × (ActualScore - ExpectedScore)
ActualScore is simply 1 on a win or 0 on a loss.
ExpectedScore is calculated using this formula:
ExpectedScore = 1 / (1 + 10^((OpponentGlory - PlayerGlory) / 400))
WinstreakBonus = min(current quest winstreak before win, 5) × 2
Glory loss increases with current Quest lossstreak. The formula is:
GloryLoss = 10 × current quest lossstreak after loss
Ranks
Squire: 1–199 Glory
Knight: 200–399 Glory
Baron: 400–599 Glory
Viscount: 600–799 Glory
Earl: 800–999 Glory
Margrave: 1000–1199 Glory
Duke: 1200–1399 Glory
Prince: 1400–1599 Glory
King: 1600–1799 Glory
Emperor: 1800–2000 Glory
Matchmaking
Matchmaking uses a player’s Match Rating rather than Glory alone. The formula is:
Match Rating = Glory + (20 × current winstreak) - (15 × current lossstreak)
