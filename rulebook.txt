FABLED
Official Rulebook
You are a magnate in the kingdom of Fantasia, home of the mystical and magical.
As an aficionado for rare artifacts, you would love to add more items to your collection. However, embarking on a quest is out of the question for an inexperienced loafer such as yourself.
Fortunately, Fantasia is host to many thrill-seeking adventurers who love nothing more than taking on dangerous quests, and you have the pocketbook to hire the best of the best. Be warned though, you are not the only wealthy collector after these rare artifacts. Your adventurers will need good teamwork and moxie to fight off other parties on the path to bringing you glory.

INTRODUCTION
Fabled is a tactical turn-based combat game focused on positioning, ability timing, and party construction. Players build parties of three adventurers to battle with and take turns using abilities or shifting positions. The battle is won when the final enemy adventurer is defeated.
GAME OVERVIEW
Players hire three adventurers to form their party.
Adventurers have the following attributes:
A Talent.
A Signature Ability.
Two Basic Abilities.
A Twist Ability.
Adventurers also have four stats:
HP, or how much health the adventurer has.
Attack, which affects how much damage the adventurer deals with abilities.
Defense, which affects how much damage the adventurer takes from abilities.
Speed, which affects initiative.
CLASSES
Adventurers belong to one of eight classes.
Melee Classes
Wardens, low-damage tanks that protect allies and absorb pressure.
Fighters, frontline damage dealers that pressure enemy frontlines.
Rogues, disruptors that manipulate positioning and create openings.
Ranged Classes
Rangers, flexible attackers that combine damage and utility.
Mages, backline damage dealers with high impact but low durability.
Clerics, supports that sustain allies through healing and cleansing.
Mixed Classes
Nobles, tanky attackers that control the game through positioning.
Warlocks, disruptors that punish enemy action and inaction.
BATTLE FORMATION
Each team uses a triangle formation with a backline behind a frontline. Both frontlines face each other with their backlines behind them.

Formation Rules
One adventurer must always be in the frontline, and up to two adventurers can be in the backline
If the frontline adventurer is knocked out, the leftmost backline adventurer moves forward.
ROUND STRUCTURE
A round consists of both players' turns, with end of round effects resolving after the second player's actions have finished resolving.
During a player's turn, the player selects one action per adventurer. Then, actions resolve in a clockwise order beginning with the frontline.
INITIATIVE
Initiative determines which player acts first each round. The player with the higher speed frontline adventurer at the beginning of a round gets initiative and takes their turn first. If tied, the previous round’s loser acts first.
A player who loses the initiative check of round 1 will get an extra swap phase that round before selecting actions in which they can swap two adventurers once.
ACTION TYPES
An adventurer may choose one of three actions:
Use an active ability.
Use an active artifact.
Swap positions with an ally, which can only be selected once per round.
If an adventurer cannot select an action, they are skipped during action execution. Players can also choose to skip an adventurer's action.
Abilities often have different effects when used from the frontline as opposed to the backline. Additionally, an ability with power is considered an attack and can deal damage.
ABILITY TYPES
There are three types of abilities: Basic Abilities, Signature Abilities, and Twist Abilities.
Adventurers can only have two Basic Abilities, chosen from a pool specific to their class, and one Signature Ability, chosen from a pool specific to that adventurer. 
Adventurers always have a Twist Ability, but only one can be used per party each battle and they cannot be used while another is active.
Abilities can also be passive or active. Passive abilities always apply their effects, whereas active abilities only apply their effects when used as an action.
ARTIFACTS
The player can bring three artifacts to a battle, which can be used for one time effects but are put on cooldown afterwards.
Artifacts can be active or reactive. Active artifacts are put on cooldown when used as an action, and reactive artifacts are put on cooldown after their effect triggers. While on cooldown, an artifact cannot be used and does not trigger.
TARGETING RULES
The targeting range of abilities changes if the adventurer is ranged or melee and if they are frontline or backline.
Melee adventurers cannot target backline enemies who aren't idle.
Ranged adventurers can target all enemies while in the frontline, but can only target frontline enemies and the backline enemy across from them while in the backline.
Spread abilities affect all legal targets, but deal 50% damage.
RANGED RECHARGE
Ranged adventurers become idle for one round after using three abilities, or two for mixed adventurers, and must recharge by skipping their action that round. While idle, they can be targeted by melee abilities.
MIXED CLASSES
Warlocks and Nobles are unique in that they are ranged in the backline and melee in the frontline. This means that their targeting rules will change based on their position, and when using an ability in the backline, it counts towards ranged recharge. However, they recharge after just two ability uses while ranged.
DAMAGE CALCULATION
Damage uses the following formula:
Damage = Power × (Attack / Defense), rounded up
Damaging an enemy lowers its HP by the damage amount. An enemy is knocked out when it takes damage that reduces its HP to 0.
STATUS CONDITIONS
Status conditions of the same type do not stack, but status conditions of the different type do.
Burn: Burned adventurers take 8% max HP damage each round.
Root: Rooted adventurers cannot swap.
Shock: Shocked adventurers have -15 speed and recharge after two abilities.
Weaken: Weakened adventurers deal 15% less damage.
Expose: Exposed adventurers take 15% more damage.
Guard: Guarded adventurers take 15% less damage.
Spotlight: Spotlighted adventurers can be targeted by melee abilities in the backline.
STAT BUFFS AND DEBUFFS
Unless stated otherwise, stat buffs and debuffs do not stack. Stat calculation uses the highest stat buff and the highest stat debuff an adventurer has.
WINNING THE GAME
Victory is achieved when all enemy adventurers are knocked out. The battle ends immediately when the final adventurer on one side is defeated.
